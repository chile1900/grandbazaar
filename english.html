about
 


Celebrated for their contagious, lively and humorous performances, the Brazillian group, Grand Bazaar, mix gypsy, Italian, Jewish, Balkan and Eastern Europe music with rock, jazz and northeastern Brazilian folk, to create their own sound, full of colors, accents and flavors. The band has already released two albums - Grand Bazaar (2014) and Grand Bazaar II (2016 - RISCO Label - a tour through traditional songs and original compositions, and a great collective celebration of joy and cultural diversity. 

The group is also part of an important collective, RISCO, a label and group of bands from São Paulo who have hugely influenced the current independent music scene in Brazil. These bands include: O Terno, Mustache e os Apaches, Charlie e os Marretas, Luiza Lian, Música de Selvagem, Pedro Pastoriz, and others. 
 
On the RISCO Label, the band released the collective álbum RISCO # 01 (2016), recorded at the Red Bull Station studios. On this record, Grand Bazaar recorded a humorous version in English and fake Russian of the song "Whiskey is Over" , originally recorded by the blues band Os Mojo Workers, also from RISCO Label. 
 
On 2016, the band recorded with the singer Fortuna an album for children called “Tchiribim, Tchiribom – Cantando pelo Mundo" at COMEP Studios in São Paulo. The album has musical direction of the composer Hélio Ziskind, renowned for his incredible works with Castle Ra Tim Bum, Cocoricó and other successful children's TV Series in Brazil. The group also works with soundtracks for theater and cinema and, in 2017, composed the original music for the film Aqualoucos by director Victor Ribeiro, distributed and produced by Paris Filmes. 
 
Grand Bazaar have performed at festivals and at venues in Brazil such as: SESC's (Pompéia, Belenzinho, Bom Retiro, etc), Festival PSICODALIA (Santa Catarina), Festival CoMa (Brasília), Festival de Ponta Grossa (Paraná), Festival Sinestesia (Rio de Janeiro), Sala Itaú Cultural (São Paulo), Vão Livre do MASP, Showcase SIM São Paulo, Mundo Pensante, Casa de Francisca, JazzB, Jazz nos Fundos, Centro Cultural Rio Verde, Casa de Noca (Florianópolis), Sesc Joinville (Santa Catarina), AudioRebel (Rio de Janeiro), Festa Barbada (Londrina), and others.
